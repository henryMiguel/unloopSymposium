UNLOOP

Symposium on Friction in Computational Design

26th of June, 2026 | Porto, Portugal + Online

About

Unloop conference explores the friction, inconvenience, and playfulness in the interaction with computational media as inducers of meaning, reflectiveness and higher-order thinking by users.

Unloop aims to question Human-Centred Design paradigms that value flow and user satisfaction as central tenets and that, despite having been contributing to the production of usable artefacts, have also been leading users to enter into hedonistic loops – a state of optimising and normalising what is already known and familiar to them.

Unloop is developed in the context of the Break the Loop research project , that aims to provide insights into how friction, inconvenience, and other playful strategies can be used to promote meaning-making and reflectiveness in users, and to design tools that designers and other stakeholders can use to access this knowledge.

Call for Abstracts

Unloop calls for extended abstracts by scholars, designers, artists, and students working within the expanded field of this theme. We invite you to submit theoretical, praxis-based or experimental research work, considering, but not limited to, the following:

  • Affordances
  • Emotional Design
  • Flow
  • Friction
  • Heuristics
  • Human-Centred Design
  • User-Centred Design
  • More-than-Human Design
  • Persuasive Design
  • Playfulness
  • Pervasive Interaction Design
Submit extended abstracts (1.000 to 1.500 words, excluding references), in English, following the formatting in the provided template. As reviews of submissions will be double-blind, please ensure the file is completely anonymised (i.e., please omit author-related information and explicit self-citations). Accepted abstracts will be invited to hold a 15-minute presentation at Unloop and to submit a full paper for a Special Issue of the Journal of Digital Media and Interaction to be published later in 2026.

Keynote Speaker

realitydisfunctions.org @laurabeloff

Laura Beloff

Laura Beloff (Ph.D.) is an internationally acclaimed artist and researcher based in Finland. She functions in-between artistic production and academic research with a core in artistic methods and practice. Beloff’s concept- and practice-driven research engages with art, humans, environment and society in affiliation with science and technology development. Beloff’s interest in recent years has focused on investigating the diminishing gap between concepts and disciplines of biology and technology. She is Associate Professor in the Department of Art & Media and Vice-Dean for Artistic and Creative Practices at Aalto University - School of Art, Design and Architecture.
